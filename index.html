Basement Dog

Poem by Crea Wilhelmi

dog fur tumbleweed drifts across the floor
the smell of damp concrete, old dreams
not mine,
his.

he sits there, whining like a rusty hinge
gas leak?
nah, the plumber said no
but the dog knows
they always know
desire for a snack then

his food is in the basement, the black hole
i tell him to go there
in the distance a door slams
echoes
he whines again pausing only at the sound of footsteps
not mine…my husband?
no, he’s upstairs

he's thirty pounds of pure “don’t touch my squeaky toy”
but the basement is the enemy
another whine
i shoo him again
down the stairs, he went
what finally did it?
chicken nuggets?
he pauses, frightened by another sound…

just the hum of the fridge…
then the whispers…
from the basement…
they’re saying…
”Treats are in the pantry”

wait...WHAT IS THAT?? AHHHHHHhhhhhhhhh.....
